# Translation

Apisense automatically translates content between the languages supported by the app. This lets an international team work on the same apiary, each person in their own language — with no manual rewriting of notes.

______________________________________________________________________

## 1. Why translations in the app

Beekeeping is more and more often a team activity: the apiary owner, a seasonal helper, a vet, an advisor. Sometimes an international team handles a larger operation, or an apiary is shared with a collaborator from another country.

Without automatic translations, every note, inspection description, and message would have to be rewritten by hand. With translations:

- You write in your own language.
- Your collaborator sees the content in their own language.
- The content in the database stays as **one** — the translation is generated dynamically.

______________________________________________________________________

## 2. What gets translated

Automatic translations cover **text content entered by users** in specific places in the app — including inspection descriptions, notes, labels, and short messages shared within a team.

Translations **do not cover**:

- private account data (first name, last name, email);
- apiary and hive names (they stay exactly as you typed them);
- photos, audio, and video files (Apisense works on text only);
- content you explicitly mark as original (for example, quotes from documents).

______________________________________________________________________

## 3. How to set the app language

You pick the interface language and the language of your translations in the **account settings** in the app. Configuration details are in the [App Manual](../manual/app-manual.md).

Apisense supports several European languages (including Polish, English, German, Italian, French). The list of available languages may grow — the most current version is always visible inside the app itself.

## 4. End-to-end example

!!! example "Note written in Polish, read in English"
    **You (Poland):** in the app you add a note to hive 4 in the Linden apiary:

    > „Pszczoły agresywne, ramki wilgotne. Sprawdzić wentylację i poziom kondensacji. Plan: jutro kontrola ramek czerwiu."

    **Collaborator (United Kingdom):** sees the same note in their app in English:

    > "Bees aggressive, frames moist. Check ventilation and condensation level. Plan: tomorrow inspect brood frames."

    The original of the note stays in Polish — Apisense translates it automatically on display.

______________________________________________________________________

## 5. Limits

- **Regional terminology** — some regional beekeeping expressions may be translated literally rather than idiomatically. If you spot a wrong translation, rephrase the original to something more universal or report the issue.
- **Very long texts** — translation may be slightly slower for very long notes.
- **Acronyms and proper names** — acronyms (e.g. "LTE", "GPS") and apiary/hive names are left untranslated to avoid confusion.
- **Translations do not replace editing** — if a note is going to be published, check it with a native speaker.

If you are unsure how accurate a specific translation is, you can always ask the [AI Assistant](assistant.md) to explain a phrase — the Assistant also supports conversation in multiple languages.
